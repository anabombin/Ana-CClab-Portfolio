Description

Lost object description by: Pree Manogaran

This is a delicate, circular, gold band. The band is slim and hosts a single row of diamonds encrusted along one side (the side facing outwards). On that same side, the band forms a V-like curve before returning to its regular circular shape. The band measures 1/16 of an inch in thickness.

Process

Upon reading the description of the object for the first time, I decided to draw it out so that I could really picture it's shape and size. I came up with several iterations based on the p5 canvas shape, p5 tools like the curve, etc. I initially attempted to draw the object out on its own by flattening the shape into a 2 dimensional plane, and using several arc tools. However, the final drawing did not depict the lost object very clearly.

Because of its simplistic shape and little detail, I decided to place the object in its natural environment to make it more clear what the object is.

Once I had an idea that I could put onto the canvas, I placed a grid on top of the drawings to make it easier to work out the x and y axes of each shape on the canvas. The overall process was very repetitive, so, to keep track of all the individual shapes and be able to edit them further down the line; I made a comment before each one to identify them. This made the whole process much faster.

Reflection

During the initial stages of the project I was slightly struggling with the outlines of the shapes and making sure that the lines were visible. It took me several attempts to figure out that because of the looping effect of the 'draw' function, I had to specify whether I wanted and outline and a specific color before the actual shape. Once I figured that out, it was easier to continue as much of the process was based on copying and pasting different elements.

Additionally, when drawing a new shape like the arc, I kept of going back and forth to the p5 reference page because of all the components needed to draw the shapes
