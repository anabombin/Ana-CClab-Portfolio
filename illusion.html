Optical Illusion

Process

Description

My initial idea for the optical illusion was to have a tunnel like illusion created by lines that started from each side of the canvas and moved together with the mouses' position. To give the illusion of a tunnel, my initial thought was to create horizontal and vertical lines that would decrease in shape as they neared the center of the canvas to give the illusion of space dicreasing.

Process

Through several attempts and itterations, I came to the conclusion that perhaps the idea of adding horizontal and vertical lines was too complex. I was able to get them anchored to the top right corner of the illusion pretty quickly, however, once I started to anchor the end points to the opposite corners, the lines would not align properly with the illusion. In the picture above, you can get an idea of how I intended the illusion to work, however, there was only one point of the scree where they alligned properly. I also attempted to center the canvas, however that made it more difficult as I was then dealing with negative numbers. Below is a picture of ongoing calculations to attempt to figure out how to anchor the lines to the illusion.

Ultimately, I deicded to attempt to make the illusion in a different way. By using the initial functions of lines going towards the mouse, I played with colors through if statements and mod. Finally, as I had made functions that could easily be replicated, I decided to superimpose several iterations that together, appear to be reflections of one another and make intricate shapes.

Process Images

Reflection

Though I was not able to recreate my initial designs exaclty, I do feel that I have created an illusion that sufficielty represents me and all aspects that make up who I am. I do hope however, that I am able to execute my original idea at some point.

When creating and adding color to the stroke of the lines, the illusion reminded me of the rows of lights at concerts that move up and down to create patterns between them, sometimes alternating colors and often times overlapping to the sound of the music.
