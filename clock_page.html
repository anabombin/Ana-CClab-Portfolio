Experimental Clock

Process

Description

This clock represents the light and time difference between my home and NYC. Growing up, our days revolved around the hours of light instead of the time on the clock. During summer, having sunsets at 10pm represented our lively culture. On the other hand, experiencing 4pm sunsets generates a feeling of sadness and low energy. Through this clock, I'm also able to feel closer to home as it keeps both times present at all times.

Process

My idea was always to tell time through light or color because of the way I grew up. Several of my initial iterations told the time of both NYC and Spain. As someone who is often calculating the time back home, adding and subtracting times, I wanted one same screen where I was able to see both. However, my final design is the one that I felt embraced the light factor as well as the time difference in a simple but clear way.

I really liked the way the NYC clock hand for my second idea turned faster around the clock than that of the Spanish time. While the NYC time had to go around the clock twice to show that time is only read from 1-12, European time only had to go around once at it is read from 0-24. It had the double meaning of how time moves so much faster in NYC than back home.

Reflection

The overall design process felt very repetitive. Even with "if" statements and variables, there was a lot of copy paste and an unnecessarily long code. It made me wonder what other alternative ways of writing the code there are. I am sure that with the use of loops or functions, the code would be a fraction of the length. However, I was able to still make the clock as it was initially designed which is what is most important. Were I to make it again, I think I would try to make the colors fade into each other as time passes instead of having a static change every hour.
