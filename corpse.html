Exquisite Corpse

Process

Description

My initial corpse was a "sevillana" which is a dancer, who wears the traditional flamenco attire from Spain and dances to flamenco music. It is a symbol of my culture and one that is very close to me personally.

Process

From each of my peers I was given another drawing. The first of which is a plant with lush green leaves and stem, and a poop emoji for a head. The second drawing is of a goose, white elongated body with two wide orange palmate feet.

Once we had all drawings, I was given my drawing's head, Trinity's plant's body, and Yessi's duck's feet.

Because of the oddly shaped stem and feet, I figured that the best tool to use would be a curvedVertex, which would allow me to make continuous shapes with curved edges all along, making seamless forms. First was the stem, next the left foot, and then the right foot. Having made everythin into functions, it made everything much easier. All I had to do to make the right foot was invert the x positions. However, I did have some trouble when calculating where to put each vertex point. Having translated the canvas definitely helped, but I sitll had to figure out each point in relation to the width and height so that the shape would remain if the canvas was changed.

Reflection

To make the drawing dynamic, I wanted to add movement to a certain function by creating variables. However, befcause of the vertexes of each function, It made the process a littl tricky. If I were to make this project again, I would try and solve the issue of the head not moving with the rest of the body when the canvas is distorted.
